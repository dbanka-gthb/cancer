Tribal ashram schools in Palghar

Girls in residential ashram schools travel 50–75km for basic care. They are among the most underserved communities in Maharashtra and the least likely to access a government health facility independently.

🎯

School-based delivery reaches them

By bringing vaccination camps directly to the schools, we eliminate every barrier — transport, awareness, cost, and time. No girl needs to travel anywhere.

💉

The clinically optimal age window

WHO and NTAGI recommend ages 9–14 as the optimal window for HPV vaccination. Girls in this cohort mount a stronger immune response than older adolescents — meaning earlier vaccination means stronger lifetime protection.

India's national HPV campaign launched February 28, 2026 — targeting 14-year-olds only at government facilities. Maharashtra's statewide drive (March 8) also targets 14-year-olds only. Our initiative fills the gap for girls aged 9–13 in tribal ashram schools who won't be reached this cycle.

Our story

Why I built this

I spend my days at work understanding why innovative medicines don't reach the patients who need them — navigating pricing, market access, and the commercial systems that determine who gets treated and who doesn't.

Se Pehle Suraksha started from a simple realization: a vaccine that prevents cervical cancer already exists and can protect girls for life. The girls in Palghar's ashram schools don't have it — not because of science, but because of last-mile access gaps that money and coordination can close.

India's government just launched their HPV campaign — for 14-year-olds only. We are making sure the 9–13 year olds in tribal ashram schools don't get left behind.

Questions

Common questions

Is it safe to vaccinate girls aged 9–13? +
Yes — WHO and India's NTAGI both recommend ages 9–14 as the optimal vaccination window. Girls in this age group mount a stronger immune response than older adolescents, making early vaccination clinically superior, not just acceptable.
How is this different from the government's campaign? +
India's national HPV campaign launched February 28, 2026 targets only 14-year-olds at government facilities. Maharashtra's statewide drive (March 8) also targets 14-year-olds only. We are reaching girls aged 9–13 in tribal ashram schools in Palghar — a cohort and geography the current rollout does not cover.
Can I donate from the US? +
Yes — US donors can give in dollars through GiveIndia's US portal, which is tax-deductible. Email bankadarshana@gmail.com for the direct giving link and a receipt.
Which vaccine is being used? +
We use Gardasil, a quadrivalent HPV vaccine by Merck — the same vaccine used in India's national government campaign. It is approved by India's drug regulator and covers HPV types 16 and 18, which cause over 80% of cervical cancer cases in India.
How will my donation be used? +
100% of donations go toward vaccine procurement, licensed medical staff, cold chain logistics, ambulance on standby, documentation, and community awareness. Admin costs are capped at 10% of the total budget.
How do I get involved beyond donating? +
We welcome Lions members, healthcare professionals, and community volunteers. You can also share this page — every share reaches potential donors. Email bankadarshana@gmail.com to discuss collaboration.
